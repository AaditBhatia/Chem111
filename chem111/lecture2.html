  • Atoms vs Molecules:
    Atoms-Smallest chemical unit of matter or indivisible unit of an element. Held together by intermolecular forces of attraction. IM forces determine whether an atom is solid, liquid or gas Molecules- Molecule formed when 2 or more atoms join chemically. O(g)+O(g)->O2. Five diatomic molecules are Oxygen, Hydrogen, Nitrogen, Fluorine, Chlorine

  • Compound- Comination of at least 2 or more atoms of diff elements bonded together in definite ratio. Can be broken down using chemical means. Compounds broken down into other compounds, or elements. All compund are molecules nut not all molecules are compunds

  • Pure Substances- A Collection of matter with same chemical composition throughout. Consists of only one type of element or single compound. Unique physical propeties. Physical techniques(heating, filtration,....) cannot be used to extrct individual species.

  • States of matter- Physical states of substances can be converted from one form to another by addition or removal of energy. The state of a substance is called a phase, such as the liquid phase or solid phase. Physical/state change do not alter chemical composition.

  • Macroscopic properties of states directly related to arrangement and properties of particles at atomic level
    State Packed Intermolecular forces Kinetic energy
    Solid CLosely packed Strong intermolecular forces Least Kinetic energy of molecules
    Liquid Intermediately packed Intermediate intermolecular forces Intermediate Kinetic energy of molecules
    Gas widely spaced Weak intermolecular forces Greatest Kinetic energy of molecules

  • Chemical change- involves change in chemical composition of the material. Iron turning to rust in the prescence of air.
